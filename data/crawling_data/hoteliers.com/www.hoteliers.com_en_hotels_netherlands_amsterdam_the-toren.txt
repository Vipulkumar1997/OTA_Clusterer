The Toren is prominently placed amongst the grand 17th century canal houses of the Keizergracht. They form a perfect backdrop to the striking transformation of the hotel. Well known interior designer Wim van de Oudeweetering took charge of creating a chic, theatrical and slightly mystical ambience, in which style, warmth and comfort unite. The 40 guest rooms are all different and vary from small and elegant to spacious and stunningly beautiful. The choice of room resides with the guests; all will find however a hotel atmosphere which inspires feelings of relaxation and well being.

The hotel has 40 luxurious and comfortable rooms, with Special deluxe rooms and Suites with Jacuzzi for two. All rooms are equipped with all modern facilities including air conditioning and wireless Internet.

Additional information about The Toren

The practical Single rooms with shower are charmingly furnished and attractively priced. If you would like more space you should opt for a Double room (for single use) with bath and shower. Or treat yourself to a room with canal view or Deluxe room, offering the opportunity to enjoy in a whirlpoolbath and a view of the canal or garden. Extra luxury are offered by the Special Deluxe rooms and Suites with a Jacuzzi bath for 2!
Amsterdam Central Station is a ten minutes walk from the hotel and public transport to the most popular museums is located at only 100 meters from the hotel. Dam Square with the Royal Palace is considered the heart of the city. On and around the Dam you will find the Bijenkorf, Magna Plaza and the Kalvertoren. Just behind the hotel begins the famous Jordaan.
Here you will find numerous small restaurants, bars and shops.
Unless otherwise indicated, the room rates exclude breakfast which is optional and served at €14 per person. Starting January 2018 this will be €18 per person. Children under 6 years are free of charge, for children between 6 and 12 years a 50% reduction applies and from 12 years onwards the adult fee applies.
All rooms offer free Wifi and free Nespresso.

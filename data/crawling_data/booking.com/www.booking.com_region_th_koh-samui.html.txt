Koh Samui Highlights









Chaweng Beach

Chaweng‚Äôs beach is the biggest and most vibrant on the island, with powdery white sand, pubs and fine dining.








Angthong National Marine Park

Sailing through its lush green archipelago, the "Golden Bowl" is truly heaven for eco-adventure lovers.








Fisherman's Village

The streets of this charming fisherman‚Äôs village are lined with wooden Chinese shop houses, coffee shops and restaurants.








Big Buddha

Locally known as Wat Phra Yai, this modern Buddha stands 15 metres high against a background of the sea and sky.








Grandfather and Grandmother Stone

Known in Thai as Hin-Ta and Hin-Yai, these rocks are shaped like genitalia and attract plenty of giggling tourists.








Chaweng Boxing Stadium

Fancy an adrenaline-inducing spectator sport? Drop by Chaweng Stadium for a Muay Thai boxing match, complete with a live commentator.








Jungle Club

Watch busy Chaweng from above on the Jungle Club‚Äôs chilled-out terrace over drinks and a tasty meal.








Na Muang Waterfall

This is Samui‚Äôs tallest waterfall, falling 30m over rocks, with a great pool for swimming at its base.








Full Moon Party, Koh Phangan

Just across from Ko Samui, Hat Rin beach on Ko Pha-Ngan hosts the world-famous Full Moon parties.








Diving Paradise, Koh Tao

This small island near Ko Samui is jam-packed with incredible diving sites and caters for all levels.

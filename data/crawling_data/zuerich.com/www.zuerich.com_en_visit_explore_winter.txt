Hot Chestnuts























If you work up an appetite during a stroll through the city, on many corners you can find hot chestnuts roasted on a charcoal grill. Simply follow your nose!









In the Zurich region, sports enthusiasts have a great choice of activities in winter, too. Whether tobogganing, downhill or cross-country skiing, or snowshoe trekking: visitors can reach the snow-covered mountains and winter landscapes around the city in no time at all.









Toboggan



Learn more





If sufficient snow has fallen in Zurich, a 3.1km (2 mile) long toboggan run is opened on the city’s home “mountain”, the Uetliberg. Then the local buses and trams are suddenly filled with people carrying toboggans and skibobs.















Skiing



Learn more





Skiing is also no problem. From Zurich, skiers and snowboarders can reach a number of ski areas by public transportation in less than 90 minutes.















Snowshoe Trekking



Learn more





In addition, the region around Zurich offers plenty of opportunity to pursue winter sports. For example, snowshoe trekking on the Zugerberg.















Cross-Country Skiing



Learn more





Sports enthusiasts can also engage in cross-country skiing in many winter sports areas around Lake Zurich. Such as here, with the impressive Einsiedeln Monastery in the background.

Many ski areas can be quickly and easily reached from Zurich. They lure guests with their beautiful ski slopes, toboggan runs and winter walking paths – not to mention their fresh air and magnificent views.

Ski Areas Near Zurich









Mountain Hut Magic



Learn more





When it is bitterly cold outside and the snow crunches under your feet, there are a host of snug locales in Zurich where you can get together with friends and acquaintances.















Wirtschaft Degenried



Learn more





It could hardly be more romantic – here, in the middle of a forest high above Zurich, couples, friends and families can tuck into Swiss culinary classics.















Baracca Zermatt



Learn more





Kloten’s inviting urban park is home to a little piece of Alpine world. The Baracca Zermatt whisks guests away to the Valais Alps with its fondue and raclette variations.















Brustenegg–Atzmännig Mountain Hut



Learn more





Just a five-minute walk from the Atzmännig valley station, fans of alpine chic can find this cozy mountain hut, with its spacious sun terrace.























In winter, it can be bitterly cold in Zurich. On days like this, there is nothing better than stopping off at one of these cozy cafés and relaxing over coffee and cake.









Cozy Cafés in Zurich



Find Out More




















These cozy cafés are the perfect place for warming up both body and soul on freezing winter days.














In winter, it is well worth taking a walk along Zurich’s lakeside promenade. On clear days, you can see as far as the snow-covered Swiss Alps in the distance.








The wooden bridge between Rapperswil-Jona and Hurden is part of the Way of St. James and leads past the Heilig Hüsli pilgrimage chapel. Even without going on a pilgrimage, this path is truly magical.











Wellness Oases in Zurich



Learn more





When it is snowing outside and the temperatures are icy cold, what could be better than a relaxing day of pampering. Fortunately, there are many wellness oases in Zurich and surrounding region where you can chill out and recharge your batteries.















Lakeside Sauna



Learn more





The sauna at the Seebad Enge lido is not for the fainthearted. For after sweating it out in the sauna, hardy guests cool down by jumping into the cold Lake Zurich.















Thermal Baths & Spa Zurich



Learn more





Nowadays, the former brewery is a place where people chill out, bathe and sweat. Here guests can let their spirits soar as they relax in old wooden tubs or on the beautiful roof terrace.















Novum Spa



Learn more





Baden is home to the most mineral-rich thermal springs in Switzerland, and is renowned for its bathing culture. The spa at the Hotel Limmathof is beautiful and definitely worth a visit.




























Get out of the city, switch off and delight in the peace and quiet – on a walk through the forest in the Sihlwald conservation area above Lake Zurich.








The lake promenade in Rapperswil-Jona, too, invites visitors to enjoy a relaxing winter stroll. Afterwards, they can warm up over a hot chocolate in one of the Old Town cafés.











Events in Winter




One of Switzerland’s largest New Year’s Eve parties is held in Zurich: at the New Year’s Eve Magic festivities, the firework display high over Zurich’s lake basin enchants around 100,000 visitors every year.















Ice Hockey




Switzerland is an ice hockey nation. In winter, the Swiss Championships are held, among other places, at the Hallenstadion indoor stadium in Zurich, where the locals cheer on the ZSC Lions!















Art on Ice




The world’s best figure skaters and international music stars come together every year at the popular Art on Ice ice skating gala.

Trip highlights
Travelling in the company of renowned Sydney chef and TV personality Peter Kuruvita
Immersing yourself in the food, spices and culture of Sri Lanka
Cooking curry in a locally crafted clay pot over an open fire
Staying in some of Sri Lanka's most exquisite boutique hotels
Exploring the ancient ruins at the old capital Anuradhapura
Time spent in the hill capital of Kandy and the Royal Botanical Gardens
Viewing large herds of Elephants on a jeep Safari of Uda Walawe National Park
High Tea on the lawn at The Grand Hotel in Nuwara Eliya
Cooking Hoppers and Kottu Roti with village elders in Kandy
Uncovering historic Galle
Trip escorted by Peter Kuruvita
Join renowned Sydney chef, author and TV personality Peter Kuruvita on this extraordinary food safari to Sri Lanka. From street food and egg hoppers to tea inspired dinners at some of Sri Lanka's finest dining establishments, Peter will take you behind the scenes to meet the people and discover the spices that make up Sri Lanka's melting pot and which first ignited his passion for food. From the palm fringed west coast, we travel to the ancient capital Anuradhapura to visit its world heritage listed ruin sites. We continue to the rock fortress of Sigiriya  before heading to the hill town of Kandy with its splendid botanical gardens and lush tea plantations.  We spend two nights in the luxurious Lighthouse Hotel in Galle designed by the late Geoffrey Bawa and in the Uda Walawe National Park we enjoy a little luxury on the edge of the national park. Along the way we will try our hand at making egg hoppers and kottu roti as well as cooking our curry lunch in clay pots over an open fire. These are but a few of the incredible food experiences you will have on this truly inspired journey. This is a food safari like no other. We hope you can join us!

Day 1
Arrive Colombo
Arrive into Colombo just prior to midnight (recommended flight is SQ468 departing Singapore at 2240 and arriving into Colombo at 2355). You will be met and transferred to your hotel in Colombo (arriving to hotel at approx 02:00 am). Overnight Cinnamon Lakeside Hotel.
Meals:
Nil
Day 2
Pettah Market and City sights
In the morning you will have an opportunity to rest before meeting your guide at around 10am. You will have a group briefing (Peter will join you for dinner this evening), before heading out to the bustling Pettah Market to discover the spices and flavours of Sri Lanka. En-route your guide will point out some of the historic sights and landmarks of Colombo. Lunch will be enjoyed at a local restaurant near Pettah Market. You will return to the hotel where you will have the remainder of the afternoon at leisure. Tonight join Peter for an authentic Sri Lanka welcome dinner at the exquisite Nugagama Restaurant in the Cinnamon Grand Hotel. Overnight Cinnamon Lakeside Hotel, Colombo.
Meals:
B,L,D
Day 3
Drive to Anuradhapura
Today we leave for the drive to Anuradhapura, the first capital of Sri Lanka. Anuradhapura, the most celebrated of Sri Lanka's ancient ruined cities, dates back to the 5th Century BC.  Our lunch en-route today will be at the local Saruketha Restaurant where Peter will introduce you to some of the local flavours of the region. Overnight Palm Garden Village, Anuradhapura.
Meals:
B,L,D
Day 4
Morning sightseeing of sacred city of Anuradhapura, travel to Habarana
Today we visit the Sacred City of Anuradhapura. We will take in many sites of archaeological and historical interest including Isurumuniya (the first temple to be built in the island), Sri Maha Bodhi Tree with an authenticated history of more than 2300  years and which was planted from a sapling from the original tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment and Ruwanveliseya, the first stupa built in Sri Lanka. After our morning exploration of Anuradhapura we will drive to Habarana where you will be transported in tuk tuks to the Lake and then taken across the Habarana Tank in a canoe to a local farmer's village. A traditional lunch will be taken served in lotus leaf with an arrack cocktail enjoyed out of coconut shell. Overnight Chaaya Village, Habarana.
Meals:
B,L,D
Day 5
Explore the rock fortress of Sigiriya, drive to Kandy
This morning we have an opportunity to climb the rock fortress of Sigiriya, a Royal Citadel for 20 years in the 5th Century AD and built by King Kasyapa. This unique rock fortress or "castle in the sky" is a massive monolith of red stone that rises 600ft above ground. We then leave for Kandy - the hill capital of Sri Lanka.  We will drive to Matale where we will take a walk in a Spice Garden and enjoy lunch and a cooking demonstration by Peter to deepen our knowledge about the growth and use of spices in the day to day life. In the early evening we will head out to watch a Cultural Performance by traditional Kandyan Dancers and Drummers including devotional "Fire Walking".  Overnight Mahaweli Reach, Kandy
Meals:
B,L
Day 6
Sightseeing in Kandy and learning the art of making Kottu Roti and hoppers
Sri Lanka's hill capital, stronghold of the Sinhala Kings, Kandy is the country's most beautiful town. Kandy is 488 metres above sea level and next to Colombo is Sri Lanka's most visited place. This morning we visit the Royal Botanical Gardens at Peradeniya where we will enjoy the botanical marvels of this superb setting (one of the finest of its kind in Asia with more than 5000 species of flora, and once the headquarters of Lord Mountbatten). The Gardens began as a pleasure garden for a Sinhala King. We will enjoy a picnic lunch the Gardens and listen to stories of days gone by from our hosts. Following lunch we will take in some further sightseeing of Kandy including the golden roofed Dalada Maligawa where the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha is enshrined. We will return to our hotel for a rest. In the late afternoon/early evening Peter will join with local elders and everyone will have the opportunity to partake in a hands on cooking class where you will make the delicious and traditional kottu roti and famous egg hoppers. The results will be enjoyed as your dinner! Overnight Mahaweli Reach Hotel, Kandy.
Meals:
B,L,D
Day 7
Train journey to Narnu Oya and transfer to Langdale Bungalows
Today we depart Kandy and have a different experience all together, taking a local train from Peradeniya Kandy to Narnu Oya. Our journey is a fantastic way to experience old world Sri Lankan travel and a fabulous way to take in the stunning scenery of Sri Lanka's hill country. Once at Narnu Oya you will be met and transferred to the beautifully situated Langdale Bungalows in time for High Tea. Tonight you will enjoy an exquisite dinner prepared by Peter and the chefs at Langdale. Overnight Langdale Bungalows
Meals:
B,D
Day 8
Tea Plantations and Nuwara Eliya
Today transfer to Nuwara Eliya, also known as "Little England". En-route we will visit a fantastic local vegetable market as well as visit a working tea factory and plantation. You will witness the manufacturing process of world famous "Ceylon Tea" and taste a pure unblended cup of fresh tea. Nuwara Eliya is a great place to explore on foot and you may like to visit sites like Victoria Park, Gregory Lake, the local market and Golf Course. In the late afternoon traditional Grand Hotel High Tea will be taken overlooking the beautiful lawn. Overnight Grand Hotel, Nuwara Eliya.
Meals:
B,D
Day 9
Single Tree Walk
Today we enjoy a fantastic walk up along the edge of Lake Gregory. The walk begins by climbing up Single Tree Mountain through tea plantations and Eucalyptus forests, before descending to the tea plantation of Shantipura Weather permitting there are some wonderful viewpoints along the way which provide a breath-taking views south to Adam’s Peak and north to the Kandyan hills. Accompanied by a naturalist guide this is a fantastic exploration and insight to the local area on foot. Overnight Grand Hotel, Nuwara Eliya.
Note: Day walk weather permitting.
Meals:
B
Day 10
Jeep safari Ude Walawe and hands on cooking demonstration
Today we depart for Uda Walawe National Park. A fantastic lunch at a nearby pottery village has been planned for today where you will cook in clay pots over an open fire and prepare a delicious lunch. Following lunch, our next stop is Uda Walawe National Park. This park is approximately 30,821 hectares in extent and lies within the Ratnapura and Monaragala Districts. It acts as the catchment to the Uda Walawe Reservoir and is located in the Dry Zone. This Park comprises grasslands and thorn scrubs and many valuable species of trees are found within it. Large herds of Elephants and Deer species such as spotted Deer, Sambhur, Barking deer and Langur, Wild Boar, Water Buffalo, Jackal are some of the prominent wild animals found in this Park and a variety of avifauna is seen. After our safari we head to the comfort of our nearby hotel. Overnight Grand Udawalawe Hotel, Udawalawe.
Meals:
B,L,D
Day 11
Travel to port city of Galle. Cooking Class and walking tour of old Fort.
After breakfast we leave for Galle on the south west coast. Galle is a port city, standing on one of the main sea routes in the southern part of the city. It has a unique charm among the many historic places in Sri Lanka. The Dutch built the 1st Protestant church in Sri Lanka here, and it is also famous for its Dutch Fort, a world heritage listed site. We will enjoy a walking tour of the historic Galle Fort and afterwards we will enjoy a cooking class. (depending on time and group size, group may be split for cooking/walking tour). After lunch you will have some time to explore the old city and surrounds at your leisure or just relax by the beach and take a dip in the ocean. The evening is at your leisure. Overnight Lighthouse Hotel, Galle.
Meals:
B,L
Day 12
In Galle - Galle Fort and Markets
Today we will have a later start with time to relax and enjoy the surrounds of the beautiful Lighthouse Hotel. We will have ample to time then to explore the Galle Fort, visit the markets and enjoy this historic seaside town.
Meals:
B
Day 13
Travel to Colombo.
Today we travel to Colombo. The afternoon will be at leisure to explore the markets, do some last minute shopping or sightseeing or just relax by the pool. Your final dinner will be an exquisite seafood grilled dinner in a private venue overlooking the pool - a fitting finale to your wonderful food safari in Sri Lanka. 
Overnight Cinnamon Lakeside.
Meals:
B,D
Day 14
In Colombo. Evening transfer to the airport.
Your tour concludes after breakfast today. A late checkout will be arranged for those departing on a late evening flight. At around 6-7pm one group transfer will be provided to the airport for your departure to Australia early the following morning (recommended flight is SQ469 departing Colombo at 0110+1)
Meals:
B

About Your Leader
About Your Leader
After 3 decades as an award-winning chef and restaurateur, Peter has diversified his career to encompass the roles of TV presenter, author, industry speaker and restaurant consultant.
Peter is well known for his time at Sydney’s iconic Flying Fish Restaurant & Bar which he opened in 2004, and successfully led the kitchen team as Executive Chef for 8 years before moving on.
Peter’s love of the Pacific & passion for seafood led to the opening of Flying Fish at Sheraton Fiji Resort in 2008, listed on Tripadvisor as one of the Top 10 Restaurants in the Pacific Region.
Flying Fish Tokoriki located at Sheraton Resort & Spa, Tokoriki Island, Fiji followed in 2014.
Peter’s signature style of fresh quality seafood, stunning ambience and beachside locations can also be experienced in Australia in his home town of Noosa at Noosa Beach House.
2016 saw Peter’s restaurant portfolio expand across the Pacific to yet another idyllic beachfront location at The Sheraton Samoa Aggie Grey’s Hotel & Bungalows located in the center of the picturesque town of Apia. In July, 2016 Aggie Grey’s by Peter Kuruvita opened its doors.
Peter’s childhood experiences cooking with his Grandmother in the traditional kitchen of his ancestral home in Colombo, Sri Lanka inspired his first cookbook “Serendip – My Sri Lankan Kitchen”.
His next cookbook “My Feast with Peter Kuruvita” takes food lovers on a culinary and cultural journey through the island communities of Asia and the South Pacific.
Peter has now presented 4 TV series produced by The Precinct Studios for SBS Television, which air on many networks worldwide.
His shows combine beautiful scenery with sensational local cuisine

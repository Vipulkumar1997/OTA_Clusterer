INTRODUCING ST. VINCENT AND THE GRENADINES


Escape to these exotic islands for the best of both worlds: St. Vincent is bustling with life and soul, while the Grenadines offer a perfectly tranquil atmosphere. Travel here for carnival season in June and July and experience island life at its fullest. Watch the world-famous yacht regatta in January. For a taste of luxury yourself, venture out from your beach resort on a private sailing trip. Or simply stretch out on the pristine sands and watch the waves.






Sensational beaches and Caribbean spirit, with thirty-two islands to choose from.

PRACTICAL INFORMATION


Capital city:
Kingstown.

Currency:
Eastern Caribbean dollar (in 10, 20, 50 and 100 notes).

Cuisine:
Expect to find an abundance of fresh fish in the cuisine of St. Vincent and the Grenadines.

Tipping etiquette:
A 10% service charge is usually added to your bill.

Saying hello:
Simply use “hello” and a warm smile.









WHEN TO GO


Warm, tropical climates grace St. Vincent and the Grenadines all year round, which makes island-hopping a dream. The rainy season – July being the wettest month – is often welcomed; it’s responsible for making the landscape rich and colourful.






These islands are sprinkled with historical sites. Visit the British-built 18th-century Fort Hamilton for a flavour of the region’s heritage.

Leuven Eurostar Breaks

Eurostar Breaks to Leuven are a great value way to discover Belgium. The quick and easy automated check in and the reserved and comfortable seating ensure travelling with Eurostar to Leuven is simple and convenient. On Eurostar Breaks to Leuven travel is via Brussels, from here a connecting train will take you on the remainder of the journey to Leuven, but with a total journey time of just under 4 hours you will arrive at your destination quickly - relaxed and ready for your city break.
Read more


Leuven is a town full of history, culture, architecture and gastronomy. The Leuven Eurostar will place you right in the heart of the old university town, just 20 minutes by train from Brussels. Take a trip to the Gothic Town Hall, sample a delicious Leuven pint (or two!), experience the atmosphere of the Old Market Square and marvel at the magnificence of Saint-Peter's church. Leuven is also world famous for its beers, as the home of Stella Artois, be sure to take a tour of the brewery.
Read more


Our Eurostar Breaks to Leuven include hotel accommodation in Leuven with return travel to Leuven (via Brussels).

Going on a Eurostar Break









Minimum Check-in Time
Check in at least 30 minutes prior to scheduled departure, however please allow enough time to collect your tickets and allow for any potential delays getting to the station. If you get there early then why not treat yourself to a coffee & croissant!


Collect Your Tickets
Upon arrival at the station, simply enter your Eurostar reference number into any of the 'Quick Ticket' machines in the ticket hall or if you get stuck just ask any member of the Eurostar terminal staff who will be happy to help.


Baggage Allowance
No need to worry about checking-in your bags or how much your case weighs. There isn't a separate check-in for baggage at Eurostar as long as you haven't got more than two medium sized suitcase bags or cases and one piece of hand luggage per person. Excess baggage charges apply to any additional items and bulky luggage or sports equipment.


Seats Are Reserved
Your seats have been reserved, so there's no need to worry about booking them separately! Your seat numbers will be shown on your tickets when you print them off.


Passports & Visas
Please ensure you have a valid passport and - if required - a visa for your visit. Border formalities are completed on each departure by both UK and French immigration authorities.

Arriving in Leuven

Perfect for a cultured and relaxing break away, there are plenty of things to discover in Leuven! Don't forget to add sightseeing tickets and tours to the end of your booking. To get an idea of where the Eurostar drops you off in Leuven,
view the Leuven station map
.












Leuven Transport

Leuven buses services are run by the Belgium transport company De Lijn. The buses can be picked up from outside the train station and provide services across the city, getting you anywhere quickly and economically. From Leuven train station buses 1 and 2 will take you to the Grote Markt in the centre of Leuven.








Leuven by Foot

If you want to explore the city on the way to your hotel then many of Leuven's hotels are within walking distance of the train station. The city is pedestrian friendly with the Diestsestraat being a pedestrian-only street. By walking through the city there is plenty of time to take in the stunning medieval architecture of Leuven.








By Bike

Leuven is a very bike friendly city and hiring a bike is a great way to see the city. Bikes can be hired from outside Leuven train station where you can hop on and start exploring the city. Most attractions within the confines of the city can be reached easily on a bike. Leuven tourism office also offer a range of guided cycling tours.

Hotels near the O2 Arena London

Showcasing some of the biggest music acts and awe-inspiring sporting events across the globe, The O2 Arena London is the nation's favourite entertainment venue. Located on the Greenwich Peninsula, the iconic arena was first constructed as the Millennium Dome in 2000 and now stands as one of the finest concerts and events venues across Europe.
…
Read more




Home to an array of bars and restaurants, a cinema, night club and exhibition space, London’s O2 Arena is more than just a music venue. Surrounding the central arena is Entertainment Avenue, a hive of activity stretching as far and wide as London's New Bond Street. For a smaller scale event the IndigO2 seats 2,400 and is ideal for comedy, music or exclusive events.

When it comes to concerts, The O2 Arena London has played host to some of the biggest stars of music, sport and the stage. Opened by Bon Jovi in 2007, the arena has exceeded expectations to place itself firmly on the map as a world-class venue.

If you're planning a trip to London for an O2 event, the information below will tell you everything you need to know before you go. From hotels near the O2 Arena to top journey planning tips, visiting The O2 couldn’t be easier with SuperBreak.

O2 Arena Parking

Arrive in time your own way…



With a huge concert arena, smaller indigo venue, cinema, restaurants and exhibition spaces Parking at the O2 Arena is at a premium. The iconic venue sees thousands of visitors come through its doors everyday and anyone travelling to the venue by car, motorbike or coach is advised to pre book their O2 Arena Parking, to be sure of a space on arrival.
…
Read more




There are over 2,000 parking spaces available across the different Car Parks at The O2. From Car Park 1 which is used for the 20,000 capacity O2 Arena to Car Park 2 used for people who are visiting the bars and restaurants in Entertainment Avenue there is specific O2 Arena Parking depending on which part of the venue you are visiting and it is vital that you park in the right one. None of the car parks at The O2 are covered and parking at the venue is subject to availability.

Hide






Directions to the O2 Arena



Car Park 1

Situated 350m from the entrance Car Park 1 offers excellent access to the O2 and is the ideal O2 Arena Parking for visitors going to any concerts and events. Car Park 1 is designated for guests visiting the O2 Arena or the indigO2.



Car Park 2 & 4

For customers solely visiting Entertainment Avenue, The Bubble or Meridian Gardens parking is available at an hourly rate in Car Park 2 & 4. Car Park 2 is 300m from the main entrance, while Car Park 4 is situated 350m from the main entrance. If you are travelling by motorbike or push bike you can park for free in Car Park 2 & 4.



Coach Parking

For groups travelling down to the venue there is designated coach areas just 350m from the entrance of The O2. With 67 parking spaces available for coaches and mini buses it is best to pre book your coach parking. Heading to The O2 for a special occasion why not splash out on that special occasion and arrive in style in a Limousine, with allocated spaces in the Coach Park you can really enjoy your night.



Disabled Parking

If you are travelling to The O2 and need to park closer to the Arena due to mobility issues then there is car parking available for blue badge holders adjacent to the pedestrian entrance/exit at each of our car parks. This parking is available at a discounted rate and can be pre-booked. If any further assistance is needed on arrival at the cart park contact a Car Park Attendant either in person or through the intercom system at each car park entrance.











Transport to the O2 Arena

O2 Arena transport opportunities for less…



With a great choice of Transport to the O2 available there are plenty of ways to arrive safely at the concert venue. Whether travelling via road, rail or boat there is a great selection of O2 Arena Transport to choose from.
…
Read more




Set on Millennium Way in the North of Greenwich the O2 arena is well connected to the rest of London via National Rail services, tube lines, bus routes and the River Thames. With the opportunity to hire a bike from Greenwich you can even enjoy a picturesque cycle to the venue and leave your bike in one of the venues car parks for free.

Leaving the car behind and using the range of public O2 Arena Transport you can relax and enjoy the event you have come to see knowing that there is no struggling to get out the car park, driving home or to your hotel, or sitting in queues of traffic once the show is over.

Hide






Getting there





By bus


A number of bus routes operate to and from The O2. These include the 188 through Central London and the 129 from Greenwich centre. The North Greenwich bus station is just a short walk from The O2.





By the tube


Whether travelling from Central London or the Docklands the Jubilee Line to North Greenwich is ideal for getting to The O2, serving 27 stations across London and connecting to many other tube services.  The O2 arena is situated just 200m from North Greenwich tube station.




By Thames Clipper


Travel via the Thames and journey from the London Eye to The O2 in just 30 mins. The Thames Clipper Express is a great way to get across the River and arrive in plenty of time for your concert. Return times start from 30 minutes after the end of the concert/event.

Information about Calgary



Calgary is the biggest, cleanest, most modern and most cosmopolitan city in Alberta, Canada. It is a picturesque region that
                        is full of valleys and prairies in the foothills of the Rockies. The region is crossed by the Bow and Elbow Rivers.    Calgary
                        was the first Canadian city to host the Winter Games. It has some wonderful art galleries, the interesting Glenbow Museum,
                        Fort Macleod, the Head Smashed in Buffalo Jump World Heritage Site and the Waterton Lakes National Park, to name just a few
                        attractions. It is also the scene of country music and rodeo events.    Calgary is rich in different cultures. It’s Chinatown
                        district is one of the biggest in Canada and there is also a Little Italy. Another interesting neighbourhoods is Bridgeland,
                        while in Forest Lawn you will find lots of ethnic shops and restaurants.    The heart of Calgary is Stephen Avenue, a shopping
                        mall that has a lot of restaurants, cafés, theatres and “western” boutiques. Visit the Eau Claire market, which is very popular
                        with the locals and is close to the Prince’s Park. The most exclusive neighbourhood in Calgary is Mount Royal, which has some
                        spectacular mansions.    You can enjoy a wonderful holiday in luxury and comfort in Calgary.

Information about Muskoka - Algonquin Park



Muskoka is a Canadian region north of the Toronto metropolitan area. It takes in the towns of Huntsville, Lake of Bays, Bracebridge,
                        Gravenhurst, Georgian Bay and Muskoka Lakes.     There are good museums to visit there and lots to do in its many recreational
                        areas, parks and lakes.    North of Muskoka is Algonquin Park, which is the oldest national park in Ontario and one of the
                        biggest in Canada. It takes in about five thousand square miles of spectacular forests, hills, rivers and lakes, with wonderful
                        rocks sculpted by the movement of glaciers.     It is also rich in wildlife and an excellent place to go hiking, mountain
                        biking, fishing, canoeing, kayaking, windsurfing, sailing, bird-watching and many other sports and activities. The autumn
                        is a wonderful time to visit this region, thanks to the palate of colours in the landscape, among which shades of red are
                        predominant. You will be awestruck by the many types of birds, moose, deer, foxes, wolves and other species on show.    Enjoy
                        the spectacular scenery while you go hiking through the Skeleton Lake Fish Hatchery Trail. There’s a good range of places
                        to stay at Algonquin Park, including country homes, log cabins, camping sites and hotels.    Muskoka and Algonquin Park await
                        you in all their natural splendour.

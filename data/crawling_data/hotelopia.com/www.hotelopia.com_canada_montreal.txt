Information about Montreal



The Canadian city of Montreal is located in the province of Quebec, on the island of Montreal, on the St-Lawrence river, to
                        the east of Some of the most popular tourist attractions include the nearby Mont Tremblant Resort, in the Laurentian Mountains,
                        the Estrie wine route and Mont Saint-Hilaire. The urban Montreal is cosmopolitan, progressive and lively, while the old quarter
                        houses some fine buildings.                                                                                              
                        See the city from Mont Royal, crowned by a beautiful park and with St. Joseph's Oratory on its slopes. Don't miss either the
                        Olympic Park, the Botanical Garden and its important Insectarium. Enjoy the scenery of the port on the St-Lawrence river,
                        visit the Montreal Science Centre, the Jean-Drapeau park and different museums such as the Museum of Fine Arts, the Museum
                        of Contemporary Art or the Architecture Museum. Montreal's nightlife is very interesting. The city has a large number of cinemas,
                        theatres, discos and restaurants where you can have the most delicious international cuisine. Transport, hotel accommodation
                        and shopping here are all excellent, and, if the weather is cold, just pay a visit to the underground city, a network of 30
                        kilometres with everything you could want.     Come and visit Montreal!

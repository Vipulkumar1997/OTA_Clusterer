Accommodation In Australian Capital Territory
Are you looking for a fun-filled, pretty and interesting holiday destination away from the beaches? Are you drawn to somewhere that has more to offer than just sun, sea, sand, and surf?  If so, why not think about
New South Wales
’ inland Capital Country?  Within easy reach of
Canberra

and
Sydney
, Capital Country is one of the state’s oldest inhabited places and is filled with historical and cultural attractions. It also boasts some splendid scenery and natural attractions. There are several interesting towns, throughout the region, so why not check out our selection of Capital Country accommodation?
Things to do
Explore the beautiful Southern Highlands with heritage trails, gorgeous gardens and parks, wineries, charming villages, cascading waterfalls and lush rolling hills. The Southern Tablelands boast vast open spaces and hilly ranges. Some of the main attractions include the Big Merino and Lake George, and movie buffs will like Braidwood, which was the setting for the film Ned Kelly.  You can also spend a few hours relaxing with some fishing – a favourite spot is the lovely Wyangla Dam.
Capital Country is home to the interesting International Cricket Hall of Fame, a huge hit with sports fans, the splendid Kennerton Green, the dramatic Bungonia Gorge, and the delightful Bungendore Village Square.  Of course, the numerous attractions of the purpose built capital city are also within the region.  Some of the main cities and towns include the Canberra, the scenic rural towns of Bundanoon, Bowral, Alpine, Exeter, Mittagong, Goulburn, Marulan, Crookwell, Yass, and Robertson.   You will find a good selection of places to eat and drink, with many places serving fresh and tasty meals made with the finest locally sourced ingredients.
Getting there
Book your
flights to Canberra

or your
flights to Sydney
, as well as your Capital Country hotel for a chance to explore this scenic, rural, and diverse area for yourself.  It may be close to the modern and lively capital city, but it really is worlds apart!

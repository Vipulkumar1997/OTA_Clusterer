Read more about Playa De Las Americas



There are arguably no better resorts in Tenerife than the stunning resort of Playa De Las Americas, the hub of the island's famous nightlife. This resort in particular is one of the world's most famous nightclub bases, perfect for those who want to let their hair down and enjoy themselves until sunrise! Search through our great range of
Playa de las Americas hotels
and grab yourself a bargain break in the sun.
There's more to Playa De Las Americas holidays than meets the eye - this is also the centre of many of the paradisiacal island's great water parks, culture, zoos and reserves, as well as being second only to the capital, Santa Cruz, for entertainments. A four hour flight and short drive will take you here, and you can grab a great deal courtesy of On the Beach!
Visit the numerous aqua parks, always a favourite with the kids, Crazy Golf at Santiago, the Zoo at nearby Los Cristianos, boat trips and cruises around the coast, and even helicopter rides over the island across the mountains of Los Gigantes and Teide, Spain's highest peak. That's if you can tear yourself away from the resort's numerous beaches, both natural and man-made.
Playa de las Americas is the capital city of Tenerife's nightlife, this fun-filled resort is split into three main areas: Veronica's Strip, with it's legion of clubs and discos ready to tire the most indomitable ravers, the Starco Centre nearby, with the same youthful frolics and places to chill, and the more family-centred Patch area, with karaoke bars and pubs, lots of restaurants and pizza parlours. Don't forget that you are close to the excellent shopping and restaurants of upmarket Costa Adeje.

Playa De Las Americas Weather

There's little rain here except for the odd shower in winter, and it's permanently humid: 17° Celsius on average at New Year compared with temperatures in the late 20s° Celsius by the summer apex. The water temperature is also constantly warm, and fresh Atlantic breezes stave off any oppressive heat.

Landscape

The numerous beaches around the azure coast with beautiful fine sand make for a colourful clash with the ocean blue all around, and the huge mountains in the horizon. For spectacular sunset views, head to the main beach or the little harbour of Puerto Marina, to watch all manner of vessels from yachts to fishing boats skirt into the translucent horizon.

Culture

Tenerife's calendar is jam-packed with festivities, befitting it's Hispanic culture: from Carnivals in the capital Santa Cruz in February, to June's Music Festival. Village markets, such as the one held in Torviscas at weekends, offer a more authentic array of
Canarian
wares and goods than the primarily European-centred shopping malls in the town centre.

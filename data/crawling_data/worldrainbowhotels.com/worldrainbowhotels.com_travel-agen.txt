Certified Lesbian and Gay friendly hotels


We are pleased to announce World Rainbow Hotels, the first ever consortia program aimed at gay & lesbian  welcoming hotels worldwide.

Over 1200 gay & lesbian welcoming hotels  in over 120 destinations are participating in the program, ranging from 3 to 5  star properties located in the most popular destinations for gay and  lesbian travellers. Travel professionals will now be better equipped to  offer their clients a certified LGBT-friendly hotel.

Only carefully selected hotels have been approved into the program based on World Rainbow Hotels' strict acceptance criteria.

How to book on the GDS


Public commissionable rates

If you are looking to book a World Rainbow Hotel on the GDS, you can search for World Rainbow Hotel specific rates with the rate name of  "World Rainbow Hotels BAR". These rates will be found on the GDS in the promotional category, and are commissionable at a hotel's regular commission level.




Confidential, discounted rates

Alternatively, travel agents can book our preferred rates simply by registering online at:
http://www.worldrainbowhotels.com/travel-agents/registration
.



Simply complete the registration form to gain access to our  confidential rates. No costs apply to participate in this agreement. Our  member hotels offer both enhanced commission and discounted rates for registered agencies. It will only take you a few minutes to register.



Once we have received your registration details we will set your agency up with access to book our Discounted rates on your GDS: using the rate code GY1 (Amadeus, Sabre, Worldspan) or GAY (Galileo/Apollo)

Out and About





















World Rainbow Hotels is a global collection of gay and lesbian welcoming hotels.  To be approved into World Rainbow Hotels, hotels must meet a strict  selection criteria which analyzes the hotels location, characteristics  and category, knowledge of the local gay scene, current involvement with  the LGBT community and the hotel's compliance with non-discrimination  policies and same sex benefits for their staff. Hotels range from city centre hotels to resorts as well as a collection of small luxury and boutique properties.
